# Oracle Council submission brief

## Problem

Divination apps usually give a generic result and forget the user's lived
history. Journaling tools preserve history but rarely help a person revisit it
at the moment of a difficult decision.

## Product

Oracle Council combines required birth context with memories imported from
Obsidian, a ChatGPT Memory summary, or a ChatGPT conversation export. Birth
date, time, and place are required, while approximate and unknown details are
accepted when their precision is labelled. Ten symbolic traditions interpret
the same question. Each lens explains what it sees, cites the memory that makes
the interpretation relevant, and gives three concrete actions.

## Trust boundary

- Local processing is the default.
- ChatGPT assistant responses are excluded from memory import.
- Hidden Obsidian configuration and likely secrets are excluded.
- Users approve each memory before interpretation.
- English is the default; the complete interface and results can switch to
  Japanese.
- Uncertain birth time or place remains explicitly marked as uncertain.
- The demo does not claim to calculate a complete natal chart, BaZi chart,
  lunar mansion, or factual Moon phase.
- Results are reflective prompts, not factual predictions.
- The exported note asks what happened seven days later.

## Why it matters

The emotional result is not “the app knew the future.” It is “I finally
recognized why this decision has felt difficult—and I now have three small
ways to test what to do next.”
