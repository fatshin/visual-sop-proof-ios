Before asking the future, read your own evidence. Oracle Council starts with required birth context: date, time, and place. Exact details are welcome, but approximate or unknown information can be labelled honestly. The tool then reads selected Obsidian notes and ChatGPT memories inside the browser. The user decides which memories may be used. One question is explored through ten symbolic lenses, including I Ching, tarot, numerology, astrology, runes, and geomancy. Each lens is now a short reflective essay. It explains what the tradition notices, how uncertainty in birth time or place limits the claim, and which tension the question reveals. It compares two memories, names why the pattern may feel true, preserves counter-evidence, and ends with three concrete actions. The result does not claim a complete natal chart or a factual prediction. It turns uncertainty into a small, reversible test and a seven-day review. The reading can be saved as an Obsidian Markdown note. The magic is not that an app knew the future. It is that your past self had already left evidence you can act on.
