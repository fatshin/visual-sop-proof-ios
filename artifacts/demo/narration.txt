CostRoute Lab uses measured fixture quality and normalized cost rather than intuition to choose a model route. Twenty cases produce task-level aggregates for three models across extraction, reasoning, and drafting. With the three-task macro-average floor set to ninety-one, exhaustive search finds a route scoring ninety-one point five with a normalized bundle cost of three point zero. That is a sixty-one percent fixture reduction against the GPT-5.6-only bundle cost of seven point seven. An incomplete task-model matrix or an unreachable floor fails closed. I used Codex with GPT-5.6 to implement exhaustive routing, verify the frontier, and review every unit and aggregation claim. These numbers are synthetic demo observations, not current billing quotes.
