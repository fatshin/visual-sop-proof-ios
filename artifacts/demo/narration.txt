Agent Policy Compiler turns operating rules into decisions an agent can execute and a reviewer can audit. This tested fixture contains five policy clauses and seven runtime scenarios. Its verified result contains three allowed actions, two blocked actions, and two actions that require human approval. Every restriction cites the exact policy sentence that caused it. If a required policy domain is missing or uses unsupported wording, compilation stops instead of guessing. I used Codex with GPT-5.6 to define the policy representation, implement the compiler and evaluator, generate boundary tests, and review the public evidence. A live GPT-5.6 extension can draft the representation, while deterministic validation remains authoritative.
