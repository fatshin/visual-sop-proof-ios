Misconception Replay checks whether a correct answer came from correct reasoning, then verifies a pre-authored synthetic response to a different problem. This fixture contains twenty fraction responses across five distinct starting problems. Five synthetic learners reach the right answer while claiming that they added numerators and denominators directly. The evaluator flags all five explanations, reports exact ground-truth recovery on this fixed set, and supplies a misconception-specific counterexample. The fixture already contains each replay answer and explanation; the product does not generate them or record a live learner attempt. A case becomes resolved only when that different replay problem has both a correct answer and matching fraction reasoning. I used Codex with GPT-5.6 to define the taxonomy, implement fail-closed evaluation checks, and review the educational boundary. This demo verifies fixed fixture responses, not durable learning.
