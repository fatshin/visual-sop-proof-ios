Before asking the future, read your own evidence. Oracle Council starts with required birth context: date, time, and place. Exact details are welcome, but approximate or unknown information can be labelled honestly. The tool then reads selected Obsidian notes and ChatGPT memories inside the browser. The user decides which memories may be used. One question is explored through ten symbolic lenses, including I Ching, tarot, numerology, astrology, runes, and geomancy. Each lens explains what it sees, shows why it connects to a real memory, and gives three concrete actions. The result does not claim a complete natal chart or a factual prediction. It turns uncertainty into a small, reversible test and a seven-day review. The reading can be saved as an Obsidian Markdown note. The magic is not that an app knew the future. It is that your past self had already left evidence you can act on.
