Decision Invalidation Ledger records not only what was decided, but who owns it, why it was chosen, and what evidence would reopen it. This fixture contains three decisions and a new evidence package. Running the evaluator leaves one decision valid, marks one at risk, and invalidates one because measured quality crossed its explicit threshold. Every result emits an owner-linked assessment event; this fixture does not claim persisted status history. If a required value, condition, source, owner, reason, or decision date is missing or invalid, the result becomes needs evidence, never valid. An empty ledger or duplicate decision IDs produce invalid input. I used Codex with GPT-5.6 to define the ledger, implement condition evaluation, and challenge missing-evidence paths. Humans approve the ledger, and deterministic conditions own the assessment.
